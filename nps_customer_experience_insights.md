# NPS & Customer Experience Insight
**Marketing & Insight Analyst Portfolio | Building Materials Distributor (sample data)**

---

## 1. Reading NPS by Branch

NPS Score = % Promoters (9–10) − % Detractors (0–6), calculated per branch.

Branch-level NPS is more informative when read alongside order volume and
revenue rather than in isolation:
- A branch with high NPS but low order volume may be performing well on
  service but has an untapped growth opportunity.
- A branch with low NPS and high volume represents the greater commercial
  risk — high transaction volume running through a weaker customer
  experience tends to surface later as churn.

**Recommendation:** Prioritise branches by cross-referencing NPS with
revenue and repeat-purchase rate, rather than ranking on NPS score alone.

---

## 2. Segment Cross-Reference

Cross-referencing NPS category against customer type sharpens the picture:

- If Contractors skew more Detractor than Retail, this points to trade-specific
  pain points (e.g. delivery timing on an active job site is more failure-prone
  than a retail collection) — worth a targeted follow-up question rather than
  a repeat of the standard 0–10 survey.
- Detractors within the Champion or Loyal segments represent the highest
  churn risk in the dataset and warrant direct outreach ahead of the next
  scheduled survey cycle.

---

## 3. Recommendations

1. **Prioritise by customer value, not complaint volume alone.** A Detractor
   flag combined with Champion/Loyal segment status should trigger immediate
   account manager follow-up — turning a passive survey into an active
   retention tool.
2. **Investigate the delivery/first-order experience specifically for
   Contractors**, given the higher downstream cost of a delayed delivery on
   an active job site compared with a retail purchase.
3. **Close the loop with Promoters, not only Detractors.** A "would you refer
   a colleague" follow-up, or a case study request, converts a strong score
   into referral pipeline rather than a static reporting metric.

---

## Executive Summary

NPS is healthy overall, but a subset of high-value Contractor accounts show
Detractor sentiment — closing that gap is a higher-leverage priority than
raising the average score incrementally across the board.
